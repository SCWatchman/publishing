## Key Research Questions
1. **Technical Feasibility**: How will OS providers verify age without compromising privacy (e.g., zero-knowledge proofs, third-party identity providers)?
2. **Data Privacy**: What are the risks of centralized age data? How does the "Safe Harbor" provision impact user rights?
3. **Market Impact**: How does this shift the burden of age verification from apps (like TikTok/Instagram) to the OS level?
4. **Constitutional Concerns**: Potential First Amendment challenges regarding anonymous speech and access to information.

## Initial Findings & Stakeholder Reactions (May 2026)

### Legislative Context
- **Bipartisan Support**: Introduced by Rep. Josh Gottheimer (D-NJ) and Rep. Elise Stefanik (R-NY) on April 13, 2026.
- **Scope**: Moves the "battleground" of age verification from individual apps to the OS level (iOS, Android, Windows, Linux).

### Stakeholder Concerns
- **Open Source Community (Linux)**: Significant concern regarding the legality of open-source operating systems under this Act. If an OS must verify age, how does a community-driven, non-commercial distribution comply? (Source: It's FOSS, Reddit).
- **Privacy Advocates**: Concerns over mandatory government ID scans or centralized biometric databases. Discussion on Reddit and Hacker News highlights the shift from "choice" (not using an app) to "necessity" (using an OS).
- **Technical Implementation**: Questions about how this affects shared devices (e.g., family PCs) and the potential for "OS-level surveillance."

### Media Coverage
- **Gottheimer Press Release**: Frames the bill as a "protection" measure for kids online, focusing on parental control.
- **Tech Community**: Highly critical, focusing on the "death of anonymity" and the burden on small OS providers or hobbyist projects.
