# The Digital Gatekeeper: H.R. 8250

The Parents Decide Act changes the rules of the internet. It moves the burden of age verification from the app to the machine. Every phone and every computer becomes a checkpoint. 

## The Mandate
Operating system providers must verify the birth date of every user. This happens at the account setup level. If the user is under eighteen, a parent must confirm the age. The OS then passes this verification to every app on the device. The app developer no longer asks for a birth date. They simply check the OS token.

## The Friction
Open-source systems face a crisis. Linux distributions do not have central providers to manage ID scans. A developer in a garage cannot verify a million users. The bill provides a safe harbor for those who comply. It offers no shield for those who cannot.

## The Stake
Privacy advocates see a trap. A mandatory ID scan at the OS level ends digital anonymity. You cannot browse without a name. You cannot speak without a record. The bill frames this as safety. Critics frame it as a permanent digital leash.
