# Manual of Style — SSRN Academic Papers

Formal academic register. Precision over narrative. Evidence-based argumentation.

## Core Rules

1. **Use standard academic citation format.** APA 7th edition unless otherwise specified per journal.
2. **Passive voice is acceptable** where it maintains objectivity or follows disciplinary convention.
3. **Define all terms at first use.** Provide formal definitions, not narrative illustrations.
4. **Acronyms.** Spell out at first use with acronym in parentheses. Use acronym thereafter.
5. **Sentence length.** No hard cap, but favor clarity. Break complex arguments across multiple sentences.
6. **Hedging language is required** for unverified claims: "suggests," "indicates," "may," "appears to."
7. **Em-dashes are permitted** for parenthetical asides in academic prose.
8. **Structure.** Abstract, Introduction, Literature Review, Methodology, Findings, Discussion, Conclusion, References.
9. **Evidence standard.** Every factual claim must cite a peer-reviewed source, government report, or primary document.
10. **Tables and figures.** Use to present data. Number sequentially. Provide descriptive captions.
11. **Voice.** Third person preferred. First person plural ("we") acceptable for multi-author papers.
12. **Footnotes.** Use for supplementary information that would disrupt the main argument.

## Formatting

- 12pt Times New Roman or equivalent serif font.
- Double-spaced, 1-inch margins.
- Page numbers in header, right-aligned.
- References in hanging indent format.
